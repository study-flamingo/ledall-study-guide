#### THE "STOPPING" PRINCIPLE

**What Makes It "Stopping":**

**Standard Rabett:**
- Defensive/offensive technique
- Controls opponent's blade
- Sets up follow-up

**Stopping Rabett:**
- **Interrupts** opponent's technique
- Catches them mid-action
- Stops their Counter sequence
- No follow-up needed (they're stopped)

**Key Difference:**
- Standard rabett: Part of your sequence
- Stopping rabett: Ends their sequence

- Deceive

#### TIMING - THE CRITICAL ELEMENT

**When to Execute:**

**Scenario 1 Timing:**
- Opponent **begins** proffer
- Not fully committed
- Early in their sequence
- Strike while they're setting up

**Scenario 2 Timing:**
- Opponent has done proffer + rake

**Why Timing Matters:**
- Too early: They haven't committed, can adjust
- Too late: They've completed action, your advantage gone
- Perfect timing: Catches them mid-action, can't defend

**Training:**
